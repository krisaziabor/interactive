Ad,

The series, called panopticon, was my attempt at reckoning with the behavioral obligations that student leaders carry at all times. They are not only observed by the administration who relies on them to set an example but of course by a younger student body. I see this as advertisement rather than art because it supports that mold instead of going against it.

Art?

Here, the subjects look straight at you, turning from objects of surveillance to agents of it. The subversion of the patterns they lived through challenges the norm instead of endorsing it.
